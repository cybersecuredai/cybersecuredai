4. Preliminary White Paper: "Evolutionary Cyber Defense Through Genetic Algorithm Intelligence"

White Paper Executive Summary

This white paper proposes the development of an adaptive cyber defense system utilizing genetic algorithms to evolve defensive strategies in real-time, addressing AFRL's need for non-traditional cybersecurity methods and machine learning applications in cyber operations.

4.1 Technical Approach

4.1.1 Problem Statement

Current cybersecurity approaches rely on signature-based detection and rule-based systems that cannot adapt to novel attack vectors. Military cyber operations require defensive systems that can autonomously evolve to counter sophisticated, adaptive adversaries in real-time without human intervention.

4.1.2 Proposed Solution: Genetic Algorithm Cyber Defense (GACD)

Core Innovation: Self-Evolving Defense Mechanisms

  • Genetic Population: Multiple defense strategies compete for effectiveness
  • Fitness Function: Success measured by threat detection accuracy and false positive minimization
  • Evolutionary Operators: Crossover, mutation, and selection create new defensive approaches
  • Adaptive Learning: System learns from each attack attempt to strengthen future defenses
