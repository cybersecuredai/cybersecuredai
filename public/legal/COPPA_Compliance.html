1. COPPA Overview and Application

1.1 What is COPPA

The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) is a federal law that protects the privacy of children under 13 years of age online. COPPA requires parental consent before collecting personal information from children and establishes specific requirements for:

  • Notice and disclosure of information practices
  • Parental consent for data collection and use
  • Access rights for parents to review child information
  • Safe harbor provisions for school-authorized services

1.2 CyberSecured AI and COPPA

CyberSecured AI serves educational institutions and may encounter student data through our cybersecurity services. Our COPPA compliance approach includes:

  • School Authorization: Operating under COPPA's school consent exception
  • Limited Collection: Minimal collection of student information for cybersecurity purposes
  • No Marketing: No collection or use of student information for advertising or marketing
  • Enhanced Protection: Additional safeguards beyond COPPA requirements

1.3 When COPPA Applies

COPPA applies to CyberSecured AI services when:

  • Providing cybersecurity services to K-12 educational institutions
  • Processing network data that may include student information
  • Monitoring educational technology platforms used by children
  • Providing security services for mixed-age environments including children

2. School Consent and Authorization

2.1 COPPA School Exception

Under COPPA's school consent provision (16 CFR Â§ 312.5(c)), schools may consent to the collection of student information when it is:

  • For Educational Purpose: Collected for educational or school purposes
  • School-Authorized: Specifically authorized by the educational institution
  • No Disclosure: Not disclosed to third parties except as permitted
  • Limited Use: Used only for the educational purpose

2.2 Educational Purpose for Cybersecurity

CyberSecured AI's services qualify as educational purposes because they:

  • Protect Educational Environment: Secure school networks and educational technology
  • Enable Learning: Ensure safe and secure access to educational resources
  • Support School Operations: Protect institutional data and operations
  • Comply with Regulations: Help schools meet their legal security obligations

2.3 School Authorization Requirements

Our agreements with schools include:

  • COPPA Compliance Clauses: Specific COPPA obligations and restrictions
  • Purpose Limitations: Clear definition of cybersecurity purposes
  • Data Use Restrictions: Prohibitions on non-educational use
  • Disclosure Limitations: Restrictions on third-party sharing

3.1 Information We Do NOT Collect

CyberSecured AI does not collect or use student information for:

  • Behavioral advertising or marketing to children
  • Creating personal profiles for non-educational commercial purposes
  • Selling information to third parties
  • Any purpose not related to cybersecurity or educational support

3.2 Minimal Collection Principle

  • Necessity Test: Collect only information necessary for cybersecurity purposes
  • Automated Processing: Prefer automated processing without human review when possible
  • Aggregation: Use aggregated and anonymized data when individual data not required
  • Temporary Processing: Process data temporarily and delete when no longer needed

4. Parental Rights and School Communication

4.1 Parental Notification

While schools provide consent under COPPA, we support transparent communication:

  • School Disclosure: Encourage schools to inform parents about cybersecurity services
  • Privacy Notices: Provide clear privacy notices for school distribution
  • Information Availability: Make information about our practices readily available
  • Contact Information: Provide contact information for parent inquiries

4.2 Parental Rights Under COPPA

Parents retain rights regarding their child's information:

  • Right to Review: Request review of child's personal information (through school)
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of child's information (through school)
  • Right to Refuse: Refuse further collection or use (through school)
  • Right to Information: Receive information about data practices

4.3 Exercising Parental Rights

  • Through School: Primary channel is through the child's school
  • Direct Contact: Parents may also contact CyberSecured AI directly
  • Verification: Identity verification required for security purposes
  • Response Time: Respond to requests within 10 business days

5. Enhanced Protection Measures

5.1 Technical Safeguards

  • Data Minimization: Automated data reduction and anonymization
  • Access Controls: Strict role-based access to student information
  • Encryption: Strong encryption for all student data at rest and in transit
  • Audit Logging: Comprehensive logging of all access to student information

5.2 Organizational Safeguards

  • Staff Training: Specialized COPPA training for staff handling student data
  • Background Checks: Enhanced screening for personnel with student data access
  • Incident Response: Specific procedures for incidents involving student data
  • Regular Audits: Regular review of COPPA compliance measures

5.3 Data Retention and Deletion

  • Limited Retention: Shorter retention periods for student information
  • Automated Deletion: Automated systems to delete expired student data
  • Secure Destruction: Secure deletion methods for all student information
  • Verification: Regular verification that retention policies are followed

6. Disclosure and Sharing Restrictions

6.1 No Third-Party Disclosure

Student information is not disclosed to third parties except:

  • School Authorization: When specifically authorized by the school
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law or court order
  • Emergency Situations: To protect child safety in emergencies
  • Service Providers: Authorized subcontractors bound by same restrictions

6.2 Internal Use Limitations

  • Need to Know: Access limited to staff with legitimate need
  • Purpose Limitation: Use only for authorized cybersecurity purposes
  • No Marketing: Prohibited use for marketing or advertising
  • No Profiling: No creation of behavioral profiles for commercial purposes

6.3 Subcontractor Management

  • COPPA Compliance: All subcontractors must comply with COPPA
  • Contractual Obligations: COPPA requirements included in all contracts
  • Regular Monitoring: Ongoing monitoring of subcontractor compliance
  • Audit Rights: Right to audit subcontractor COPPA practices

7. Age Verification and Mixed Environments

7.1 Age Verification Challenges

In cybersecurity monitoring, direct age verification is often not feasible:

  • Network-Level Monitoring: Security monitoring occurs at network level
  • School Context: Rely on school identification of student users
  • Conservative Approach: Treat all K-12 users as potentially under 13
  • Grade-Level Indicators: Use school-provided grade level information when available

7.2 Mixed-Age Environments

  • Higher Standard: Apply COPPA protections to all K-12 students when in doubt
  • Separate Systems: Recommend separate systems for different age groups when possible
  • Enhanced Protections: Apply child-level protections to all minors
  • School Guidance: Follow school policies for age-appropriate handling
