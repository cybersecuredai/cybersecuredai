2. Legal Bases for Processing

We process personal data under the following GDPR-compliant legal bases:

2.1 Legitimate Interest (Article 6(1)(f))

  • Cybersecurity threat detection and prevention
  • Platform security and fraud prevention
  • Service improvement and optimization
  • Direct marketing to existing customers (with opt-out)

2.2 Contract Performance (Article 6(1)(b))

  • Providing cybersecurity services as contracted
  • User account management and authentication
  • Billing and payment processing
  • Customer support and technical assistance

2.3 Legal Obligation (Article 6(1)(c))

  • Compliance with data breach notification requirements
  • Regulatory reporting and audit requirements
  • Law enforcement cooperation when legally required
  • Tax and financial record keeping

2.4 Consent (Article 6(1)(a))

  • Marketing communications to prospects
  • Non-essential cookies and tracking
  • Participation in research and surveys
  • Newsletter subscriptions and updates

4. Data Processing Activities

4.1 Categories of Personal Data

  • Identity Data: Name, job title, organization
  • Contact Data: Email address, phone number, mailing address
  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, device information
  • Usage Data: Platform interaction, feature usage, preferences
  • Security Data: Authentication logs, access patterns, threat indicators

4.2 Special Categories of Data

We do not intentionally collect special categories of personal data (Article 9) such as:

  • Racial or ethnic origin
  • Political opinions
  • Religious or philosophical beliefs
  • Health data
  • Biometric data (except for authorized biometric authentication)

5. International Data Transfers

5.1 Transfer Safeguards

When transferring EU personal data to third countries, we ensure adequate protection through:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): EU Commission-approved clauses
  • Adequacy Decisions: Transfers to countries with adequate protection
  • Binding Corporate Rules: For intra-group transfers
  • Additional Safeguards: Encryption, pseudonymization, access controls

5.2 Third Country Recipients

  • United States: Primary data processing location with SCCs
  • Cloud Providers: Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure with GDPR compliance
  • Support Services: Limited transfers for customer support
  • Security Vendors: Threat intelligence sharing with adequate safeguards

6. Data Retention Periods

6.1 General Retention Policy

  • Customer Data: Duration of service relationship plus 3 years
  • Security Logs: 13 months for operational logs, 7 years for compliance
  • Marketing Data: Until consent is withdrawn or 3 years inactive
  • Support Data: 2 years after case resolution

6.2 Automated Deletion

  • Automated systems delete data when retention periods expire
  • Annual reviews ensure compliance with retention schedules
  • Users receive notifications before account data deletion
  • Backup systems include automatic purging after retention periods

7. Security Measures (Article 32)

7.1 Technical Measures

  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit
  • Access Controls: Role-based access with multi-factor authentication
  • Monitoring: 24/7 security monitoring and anomaly detection
  • Backup Security: Encrypted backups with restricted access

7.2 Organizational Measures

  • Staff Training: Regular GDPR and data protection training
  • Data Processing Agreements: Contracts with all processors
  • Privacy by Design: Built-in privacy protections in all systems
  • Regular Audits: Internal and external security assessments

8. Data Breach Response (Articles 33-34)

8.1 Breach Detection and Assessment

  • Automated breach detection systems with real-time monitoring
  • Risk assessment within 24 hours of breach discovery
  • Documentation of all data breaches regardless of notification requirements
  • Regular testing of breach response procedures

8.2 Notification Procedures

  • Supervisory Authority: Notification within 72 hours when required
  • Data Subjects: Direct notification when high risk to rights and freedoms
  • Customers: Immediate notification for breaches affecting their data
  • Documentation: Comprehensive breach register maintained

9. Data Protection Impact Assessments (Article 35)

9.1 DPIA Requirements

We conduct DPIAs for processing operations that are likely to result in high risk, including:

  • New AI and machine learning implementations
  • Large-scale processing of special category data
  • Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas
  • Processing involving vulnerable data subjects

9.2 DPIA Process

  • Risk assessment methodology aligned with regulatory guidance
  • Stakeholder consultation including data subjects when appropriate
  • Mitigation measures implementation and monitoring
  • Supervisory authority consultation when high risk cannot be mitigated

10. Supervisory Authority and Complaints

10.1 Lead Supervisory Authority

For cross-border processing, our lead supervisory authority is determined by our main establishment in the EU or the location of our EU representative.

10.2 Right to Lodge Complaints

EU data subjects have the right to lodge complaints with supervisory authorities:

  • Contact your local data protection authority
  • File complaints in your habitual residence, place of work, or place of alleged infringement
  • Complaints are free of charge and can be filed without legal representation
  • We commit to cooperating fully with supervisory authority investigations
