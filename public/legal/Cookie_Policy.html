1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small data files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used by website owners to make their websites work more efficiently, provide reporting information, and deliver personalized experiences.

CyberSecured AI uses cookies to enhance your security experience, maintain session state, and provide essential cybersecurity functionality.

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several important reasons:

  • Security: Protecting against unauthorized access and security threats
  • Authentication: Managing user sessions and multi-factor authentication
  • Functionality: Remembering preferences and customizations
  • Performance: Optimizing platform speed and reliability
  • Analytics: Understanding how users interact with security features
  • Compliance: Meeting regulatory requirements for audit trails

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

5.1 Browser Controls

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies or all cookies
  • Set cookies to be deleted when you close your browser
  • Create exceptions for trusted sites like CyberSecured AI

5.2 Platform Cookie Controls

Within your CyberSecured AI dashboard, you can:

  • Access cookie management settings under Privacy Preferences
  • Enable or disable non-essential cookies
  • View detailed cookie usage reports
  • Export your cookie preferences for compliance records

5.3 Important Considerations

Security Warning: Disabling essential cookies will significantly impact platform security and functionality. For cybersecurity platforms, we strongly recommend allowing essential and security cookies to maintain protection effectiveness.

7. Cookie Security and Privacy

7.1 Security Measures

  • All cookies are transmitted over encrypted HTTPS connections
  • Sensitive cookies are marked as Secure and HttpOnly
  • Cookie data is regularly rotated and expires automatically
  • Cross-site scripting protections prevent cookie theft

7.2 Privacy Protection

  • No personally identifiable information is stored in cookies when possible

  • Users can request cookie data reports under GDPR and CCPA
  • Cookie retention periods are minimized for privacy protection
