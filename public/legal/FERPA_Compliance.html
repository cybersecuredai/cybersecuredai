1. FERPA Overview and Compliance

1.1 What is FERPA

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) is a federal law that protects the privacy of student education records. FERPA applies to all educational institutions that receive federal funding and governs:

  • Access rights to student educational records
  • Disclosure limitations for educational records
  • Directory information handling
  • Student and parent rights regarding records

1.2 CyberSecured AI's FERPA Role

CyberSecured AI serves educational institutions as a:

  • School Official: Under the school official exception (34 CFR Â§ 99.31(a)(1))
  • Institutional Service Provider: Providing cybersecurity services with legitimate educational interest
  • Technology Partner: Supporting institutional security and compliance requirements

2. Educational Records Protection

2.1 Types of Educational Records We May Access

In providing cybersecurity services, we may encounter:

  • Network Access Logs: Student login and system access records
  • Email Security Data: Student email metadata for threat detection
  • System User Data: Student account information for security monitoring
  • Educational Technology Logs: Student interaction data with educational platforms

2.2 Records We Do NOT Collect Directly

  • Academic grades or transcripts
  • Student disciplinary records
  • Personal student communications content
  • Financial aid or health records

2.3 Legitimate Educational Interest

Our access to student records is limited to legitimate educational interests in:

  • Campus Security: Protecting educational institution networks and systems
  • Student Safety: Detecting cyber threats that could harm students
  • Institutional Compliance: Supporting FERPA and other regulatory compliance
  • Educational Technology Protection: Securing student access to educational resources

4. Data Use and Disclosure Limitations

4.1 Permitted Uses

We use student educational records only for:

  • Cybersecurity Services: Threat detection, incident response, security monitoring
  • System Security: Protecting institutional networks and educational technology
  • Compliance Support: Assisting with FERPA and security compliance requirements
  • Service Improvement: Enhancing cybersecurity protection for educational environments

4.2 Prohibited Uses

We will NOT use student records for:

  • Commercial advertising or marketing to students
  • Building student profiles for non-educational purposes
  • Selling or sharing data with third parties for commercial purposes
  • Any purpose not directly related to cybersecurity services

4.3 Disclosure Restrictions

We do not disclose student educational records except:

  • To the Educational Institution: Security reports and incident notifications
  • As Required by Law: Subpoenas, court orders, lawful investigations
  • Emergency Situations: Immediate threat to health or safety
  • Authorized Service Providers: Subcontractors bound by same FERPA obligations

5. Student and Parent Rights

5.1 FERPA Rights Overview

Under FERPA, students (and parents of students under 18) have rights regarding educational records:

  • Right to Inspect: Review educational records maintained by the institution
  • Right to Request Amendment: Seek correction of inaccurate records
  • Right to Control Disclosure: Consent to most disclosures of educational records
  • Right to File Complaints: Lodge complaints with the Department of Education

5.2 How Rights Apply to CyberSecured AI

  • Inspection Requests: Forward to the educational institution as the record owner
  • Amendment Requests: Coordinate with institution for record corrections
  • Disclosure Consents: Institution maintains primary responsibility
  • Access to Our Processing: Provide information about our security processing upon request

5.3 Exercising Rights

To exercise FERPA rights related to CyberSecured AI services:

  • Contact Your Institution: Start with your school's registrar or FERPA officer
  • Contact CyberSecured AI: For questions about our data processing: ferpa@cybersecuredai.com
  • Department of Education: File complaints at studentprivacy.ed.gov

7. Data Retention and Destruction

7.1 Retention Periods

  • Security Logs with Student Data: Retained per institutional agreement, typically 1-3 years
  • Incident Response Data: Retained for 7 years for legal and compliance purposes
  • Aggregated/De-identified Data: May be retained for service improvement
  • Temporary Processing Data: Deleted within 30 days when no longer needed

7.2 Secure Destruction

When retention periods expire, we ensure:

  • Secure deletion using NIST-approved methods
  • Certificate of destruction provided to institutions
  • Verification that all copies are destroyed
  • Documentation for audit and compliance purposes

8. Incident Response and Breach Notification

8.1 FERPA Breach Response

In the event of a breach involving student records, we will:

  • Immediate Notification: Alert the educational institution within 24 hours
  • Containment: Take immediate steps to stop the breach and prevent further disclosure
  • Assessment: Determine the scope and impact of the breach
  • Documentation: Maintain detailed records of the incident and response

8.2 Institutional Coordination

  • Work with institution to determine notification requirements
  • Assist with student and parent notification if required
  • Provide technical details for institutional breach reports
  • Support any resulting investigations or audits

8.3 Prevention and Monitoring

  • Continuous security monitoring for unauthorized access
  • Regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments
  • Employee background checks and ongoing security training
  • Incident simulation exercises and response plan testing
